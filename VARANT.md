## The Name

**Varant** — from Sanskrit "vara" (वर)

Vara means: *the chosen one, the granted wish, the deliberate choice, the boon you ask for knowing you can only ask once.*

In ancient texts, when a god granted a "vara" to someone, it wasn't casual. It was irreversible. You thought carefully. You committed fully. There was no "undo."

That's exactly what Varant is about.

## The Origin Story

For 2,500 years, Indian philosophy built the world's most rigorous frameworks for reasoning under uncertainty:

- **Nyaya Shastra** — the science of logical argumentation and correct reasoning. Structured debate with formal roles, formal rules, and a formal verdict.
- **Arthashastra** (Chanakya, 4th century BCE) — the world's first systematic framework for strategic decisions under uncertainty. Still studied in business schools.
- **Mahabharata's Sabha Parva** — a council debate where multiple advisors argued opposing positions before a king made an irreversible decision. The Sabha was sacred — no decision left it without a verdict.
- **Tarka Shastra** — the art of structured debate with formal roles: *Vadin* (the one who argues), *Prativadin* (the counter-arguer), *Madhyastha* (the neutral judge).
- **Mimamsa** — an entire school of philosophy dedicated to returning, re-examining, and deepening understanding of what was decided.

**Varant is not inspired by these. Varant IS these — rebuilt for founders in 2026.**

## The Sanskrit Identity System

Every element of Varant has a Sanskrit name rooted in actual philosophy. This is not decoration — each word carries 2,500 years of meaning.

### The Council = **The Sabha** (सभा)
In ancient India, a Sabha was not just a meeting — it was a sacred deliberation space. Kings convened Sabhas before wars, before treaties, before irreversible acts. Every voice had a designated role. No decision left the Sabha without a verdict. Varant's council IS the Sabha.

### The 5 Voices of the Sabha

| Symbol | Name | Sanskrit | Root Meaning | Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 🔴 | **Vitarka** | वितर्क | Deliberate counter-reasoning | Finds every flaw, challenges every assumption, names every risk |
| 🟢 | **Asha** | आशा | Hope, the rising possibility | Finds opportunity where others see obstacle, sees momentum |
| 🟡 | **Yukti** | युक्ति | Practical applied wisdom | What actually works given real constraints and resources |
| 🔵 | **Vipaksha** | विपक्ष | The opposing position | Reframes the entire question, challenges the premise itself |
| ⚖️ | **Nirnaya** | निर्णय | The final decisive verdict | Synthesizes all voices into one structured judgment |

### The Session = **The Vichar** (विचार)
Vichar means "deliberate thought, contemplation." Every time you open Varant and begin, you are beginning a Vichar — a deliberate act of thinking, not casual chat.

### The Interruption = **The Prashna** (प्रश्न)
Prashna means "the essential question" — not a casual query, but the pointed question that cuts through pretense. In ancient debates, the Prashna was the moment that changed everything. When Varant interrupts you mid-session and asks the uncomfortable question you've been avoiding — that is the Prashna.

### The Decision Memo = **The Shastra** (शास्त्र)
Shastra literally means "a written instrument of authoritative instruction and record." Every Varant session produces a Shastra — your permanent record of how you reasoned, what you weighed, what you decided, and your confidence level. It is yours. It is permanent. It cannot be undone.

### The Judgment Log = **The Smriti** (स्मृति)
Smriti means "that which is remembered" — the body of accumulated wisdom. Your Smriti is your personal collection of Shastras: every decision you made, why you made it, and what actually happened. Over time it becomes a mirror of your judgment — its patterns, its blindspots, its evolution.

### The Review = **The Mimamsa** (मीमांसा)
Mimamsa literally means "deep critical re-examination — returning to what was decided and examining it again with new evidence." One of the six classical schools of Indian philosophy dedicated entirely to this practice. Your 30/60/90 day review is your Mimamsa.

### The Confidence Score = **The Matra** (मात्रा)
Matra means "measure, degree, extent." The Matra measures how much the Sabha agreed — a precise 10-95% measure of consensus and conviction.

### The Tension Meter = **The Sangharsha** (संघर्ष)
Sangharsha means "conflict, friction between opposing forces." When Vitarka and Asha are deeply divided, the Sangharsha is high. When the Sabha is aligned, it is low.

## The Product

### Core Flow

1. **Aarambh** (आरंभ — Beginning) — User enters their decision question + context
2. **Sabha** (सभा — The Council) — 4 voices deliberate across 3 structured rounds:
   - **Pratham Paksha** (प्रथम पक्ष — First Position): Each voice speaks independently
   - **Khandana** (खंडन — Refutation): Voices challenge each other by name
   - **Nirnaya** (निर्णय — Verdict): Chairman synthesizes a structured final judgment
3. **Prashna** (प्रश्न — The Essential Question): Between rounds, the most contrarian voice interrupts with the one question you're avoiding
4. **Shastra** (शास्त्र — The Record): Structured Decision Memo — Consensus · Tensions · Recommendation · Matra · The Unseen
5. **Smriti** (स्मृति — The Memory): Session saved permanently with Mimamsa (review) date set

- **Quit vs. Stay Sabha**
- **Pivot vs. Persist Sabha**
- **Fire vs. Fix Sabha**
- **Build vs. Buy Sabha**
